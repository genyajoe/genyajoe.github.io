What is Rakugo?

Rakugo is a Japanese verbal entertainment. A story teller called Hanashi-ka performs a story only with slight movement of his/her face and hands.

Rakugo stories can be classified by some aspects such as eras, regions and social positions of the characters. Because most of the Rakugo stories are written in Edo era (1603 - 1868) and the beginning of Meiji era (1868 - 1912), they feature in life of people at the time. The Rakugo stories made in those eras are said classical. However, some storytellers has composed new stories nowadays. Such works are called modern Rakugo.

From the view of regions, the stories belongs to either Kamigata-Rakugo or Edo-Rakugo. Kamigata represents the area around current Osaka, and Edo is the former name of Tokyo.

You can find a detailed article on Wikipedia - Rakugo.
